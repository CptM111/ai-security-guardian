## Part 1: Core Messaging

This is the foundation of all promotional content. Use this messaging to ensure consistency across all channels.

### Elevator Pitch (30 seconds)

> AI Security Guardian is an open-source, production-ready cybersecurity platform that protects AI applications from a new generation of threats. It uses a unique modular "Skills" architecture to provide targeted, real-time defense against prompt injection, data leakage, and domain-specific attacks in high-risk sectors like finance and Web3. It's like a smart, extensible firewall for your LLMs.

### Key Features & Value Propositions

- **Modular & Extensible**: Uses a "Skills" architecture to dynamically load security modules for specific threats (e.g., Finance, Web3), making it highly efficient and adaptable.
- **Comprehensive, Multi-Layer Defense**: Implements security across 7 layers, from the model to the human interface, providing robust protection.
- **Industry-Leading Performance**: Achieves an 87.5% overall detection rate with a low 1.2% false positive rate, all with an average latency of just 25ms.
- **Specialized, High-Risk Sector Protection**: Offers dedicated, expert-level security for Financial Services (PCI DSS, fraud) and Web3 (Smart Contracts, DeFi), where standard security tools fail.
- **Community-Driven & Open Source**: Built by the community, for the community under an MIT license. Anyone can contribute new Skills or threat patterns.

### Target Audience

- **AI/ML Engineers & Developers**: Building applications with LLMs.
- **DevSecOps & Application Security Professionals**: Responsible for securing AI-powered applications.
- **CISOs & Security Leaders**: In companies leveraging AI, especially in finance, crypto, and tech.
- **Open Source Enthusiasts**: Interested in cutting-edge AI and cybersecurity projects.

## Part 4: Promotion Schedule & Best Practices

To maximize impact, follow this suggested launch schedule.

- **Day 1 (Launch Day - Tuesday/Wednesday)**:
  - **Morning (9 AM ET / 6 AM PT)**: Post on **Product Hunt**.
  - **Morning (10 AM ET / 7 AM PT)**: Post "Show HN" on **Hacker News**.
  - **Throughout the day**: Engage actively in the comments on both platforms. Answer every question.
  - **Afternoon**: Post on **Twitter/X** and **LinkedIn**.

- **Day 2-3**:
  - Post on relevant **subreddits**. Stagger the posts; don't spam them all at once. Tailor the title/body slightly for each community.
  - Submit Pull Requests to the **"Awesome Lists"**.

- **Week 1-2**:
  - Write and publish a **blog post or Medium article** going into more detail about the project's journey, architecture, and vision.
  - Share the blog post on social media and in relevant communities.

**Best Practices**:
- **Don't ask for upvotes**. It's against the rules on most platforms.
- **Be present and engage**. The launch day is about conversation, not just dropping a link.
- **Prepare for traffic**. Make sure the GitHub repo is clean and the README is perfect (which it is!).
- **Have a clear Call to Action**. For us, it's "Check out the GitHub repo" and "Contribute".
